Cookie Policy

Last updated: January 1, 2025

This Cookie Policy explains how DimeRepublic ("we," "our," or "us") uses cookies and similar tracking technologies on our website. By continuing to browse our site, you consent to the use of cookies as described in this policy. We are committed to being transparent about the technologies we use and providing you with meaningful choices about your privacy.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files, typically consisting of letters and numbers, that are placed on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile phone) when you visit a website. They are widely used by website owners to make websites work more efficiently, enhance user experience, and provide analytical information to website owners.

Cookies perform various functions. Some are essential for the basic functioning of the website, while others help us understand how visitors interact with our site, remember your preferences, or deliver relevant advertising. Cookies can be categorized as:

  • Session Cookies: These are temporary cookies that expire when you close your browser. They enable the website to remember your actions during a single browsing session.
  • Persistent Cookies: These remain on your device for a specified period or until you delete them. They help us remember your preferences and settings when you return to our site.
  • First-Party Cookies: These are set by the website you are visiting (in this case, DimeRepublic).
  • Third-Party Cookies: These are set by a domain other than the one you are visiting, typically by our trusted partners and service providers.

In addition to cookies, we may use other tracking technologies such as web beacons (also known as pixel tags or clear GIFs), which are tiny graphics that help us understand how you interact with our emails and website content.

2. Types of Cookies We Use

Essential Cookies

These cookies are strictly necessary for the website to function properly and cannot be disabled in our systems. They enable core functionality such as page navigation, secure log-in, session management, and protection against fraudulent activity. Without these cookies, some services on our website cannot be provided. Essential cookies do not require your consent and are typically set in response to actions you take, such as setting privacy preferences, logging in, or filling in forms.

Analytics Cookies

We use analytics cookies to understand how visitors interact with our website, measure the effectiveness of our content, and identify areas for improvement. These cookies collect aggregated, anonymized information about which pages are visited most often, how users navigate between pages, how long they spend on each page, and whether they encounter error messages. We use Google Analytics to collect this data. The information collected is used solely to improve the functionality and user experience of our website.

Functional Cookies

Functional cookies allow our website to remember choices you make and provide enhanced, more personalized features. For example, these cookies may remember your language preference, the region you are in, or your username so you do not have to re-enter it each time you visit. These cookies may also enable features such as live chat support and video playback. While these cookies are not strictly essential, they enhance your browsing experience. Some functional cookies may be set by third-party providers whose services we have added to our pages.

Marketing Cookies

We may use marketing and advertising cookies to track visitors across websites and display relevant advertisements that are tailored to your interests based on your browsing behavior. These cookies collect information about your browsing habits, such as the pages you visit, the links you click, and the products or services you view. This helps us deliver more relevant ads, limit the number of times you see an advertisement, and measure the effectiveness of our advertising campaigns. Marketing cookies may be set by us or by third-party advertising networks such as LinkedIn, Facebook (Meta), and Google Ads.

3. Third-Party Cookies

In addition to our own cookies, we work with various trusted third-party service providers who may also set cookies on our website. These third parties are responsible for the cookies they set, and their use of cookies is governed by their own privacy policies. We do not control these third-party cookies. The following third parties may place cookies on our website:

  • Google Analytics: Used for website analytics to understand visitor behavior and improve our site. Google's Privacy Policy
  • Google Ads: Used for advertising and re-marketing purposes. Google Ads Privacy Policy
  • LinkedIn: Used for targeted advertising and analytics. LinkedIn Privacy Policy
  • Facebook (Meta): Used for advertising, conversion tracking, and analytics. Meta Privacy Policy
  • HubSpot: Used for CRM, marketing automation, and live chat functionality. HubSpot Privacy Policy
  • Calendly: Used for scheduling consultations and meetings. Calendly Privacy Policy

4. How to Control Cookies

You have the right to decide whether to accept or reject cookies. You can exercise your preferences through the following methods:

  • Cookie Consent Banner: When you first visit our website, a cookie consent banner is displayed that allows you to accept or decline non-essential cookies. You can change your preferences at any time by clicking the "Cookie Settings" link in the footer of our website.
  • Browser Settings: Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can typically find these settings in the "Options," "Preferences," or "Privacy" menu of your browser. Below are links to instructions for common browsers:
    • Google Chrome
    • Mozilla Firefox
    • Apple Safari
    • Microsoft Edge
    • Opera
  • Opt-Out Tools: You can opt out of Google Analytics by installing the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on. You can also manage your advertising preferences through industry opt-out platforms such as the Network Advertising Initiative and the Digital Advertising Alliance.
  • Do Not Track: Some browsers have a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature that signals to websites that you do not want to be tracked. Our website currently does not respond to DNT signals, but you can use the other methods described above to control cookies.

5. Cookie Retention Periods

The length of time a cookie remains on your device depends on whether it is a session or persistent cookie. Session cookies are deleted automatically when you close your browser. Persistent cookies remain on your device for varying periods depending on their purpose:

  • Essential Cookies: Typically expire at the end of your browsing session or up to 12 months.
  • Analytics Cookies: Retained for up to 26 months to allow for year-over-year trend comparison.
  • Functional Cookies: Retained for up to 12 months or until you clear your browser cookies.
  • Marketing Cookies: Retained for up to 90 days from your last visit, after which they may be renewed upon your next visit.

Please note that disabling cookies may affect the functionality of our website and your user experience.

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in the cookies we use, for operational, legal, or regulatory reasons. When we make changes, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this policy. If the changes are material, we may notify you by email or through a prominent notice on our website. We encourage you to review this Cookie Policy periodically to stay informed about how we use cookies and other tracking technologies.
