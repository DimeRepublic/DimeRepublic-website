Data Processing Agreement

Last updated: January 1, 2025

This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") forms part of the agreement between DimeRepublic ("Processor") and the client ("Controller") for the provision of services. This DPA sets out the terms governing the processing of personal data by the Processor on behalf of the Controller.

1. Scope and Purpose

This DPA applies to all personal data processed by DimeRepublic on behalf of the Controller in connection with the services provided. The Processor shall process personal data only in accordance with the Controller's documented instructions, unless required to do otherwise by applicable law.

2. Data Processing Terms

The details of data processing are as follows:

  • Nature and Purpose: Processing of personal data for talent acquisition, payroll management, performance monitoring, and communication purposes.
  • Categories of Data Subjects: Client employees, contractors, candidates, and end customers.
  • Types of Personal Data: Names, contact information, employment history, performance data, financial information, and identification documents.
  • Duration: For the term of the underlying service agreement plus any applicable retention periods.

3. Security Measures

The Processor shall implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk, including:

  • Encryption of personal data at rest and in transit using industry-standard protocols.
  • Access controls based on the principle of least privilege, with multi-factor authentication.
  • Regular security assessments, penetration testing, and vulnerability scanning.
  • Employee training on data protection and confidentiality obligations.
  • Incident response and business continuity plans.
  • Physical security measures for data centers and office premises.

4. Sub-processors

The Controller authorizes the Processor to engage sub-processors for specific processing activities. Current sub-processors include:

  • Cloud infrastructure providers (e.g., AWS, Google Cloud).
  • Communication and collaboration tools (e.g., Slack, Zoom).
  • Payroll and accounting service providers.
  • Human resources management platforms.

The Processor shall notify the Controller of any intended changes concerning the addition or replacement of sub-processors, giving the Controller an opportunity to object.

5. Data Subject Rights

The Processor shall assist the Controller in fulfilling its obligations to respond to data subject requests under applicable data protection laws. This includes:

  • Right of access by the data subject.
  • Right to rectification and erasure.
  • Right to restriction of processing.
  • Right to data portability.
  • Right to object to processing.

The Processor shall promptly notify the Controller of any data subject request received directly by the Processor and shall not respond to such requests without the Controller's prior authorization.

6. Data Breach Notification

The Processor shall notify the Controller without undue delay (and in any event within 48 hours) upon becoming aware of a personal data breach affecting Controller data. The notification shall include details of the nature of the breach, categories of data affected, likely consequences, and measures taken or proposed to address the breach.

7. Compliance and Audits

The Processor shall maintain records of all processing activities and, upon the Controller's request, make available information necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA. The Controller may audit the Processor's compliance with this DPA, subject to reasonable notice and confidentiality obligations.

8. International Transfers

Personal data may be transferred to and processed in countries where the Processor or its sub-processors maintain facilities. The Processor shall ensure that any international transfer of personal data is governed by appropriate safeguards, such as Standard Contractual Clauses adopted by the European Commission or other legally recognized transfer mechanisms.

9. Term and Deletion

Upon termination of the underlying service agreement, the Processor shall, at the Controller's option, delete or return all personal data processed on behalf of the Controller, unless applicable law requires retention of such data.

10. Contact

For questions about this DPA, please contact us at hello@dimerepublic.com.
